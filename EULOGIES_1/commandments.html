An 8.5' x 11' piece of computer paper placed inside a wooden frame.

The paper contains the following words:

THE 'TEN COMMANDMENTS' FOR MEMBERS OF THE CHURCH OF CHRIST TO SAFEGUARD THE HOPE FOR SALVATION

1. You shall not let your love for God grow cold (Rev: 2:3-5 TEV)
2. You shall not forsake your duty to worship (Heb: 10:25-27, NIV)
3. You shall not abandon the Church of Christ (Heb. 10:37-39, NKJV)
4. You shall not lost faith (Jn. 3:18, CEV)
5. You shall not live according to the flesh (I Cor. 6:9-10; Gal 5:19-21, NKJV)
6. You shall not ruin the brotherhood (I Jn. 3:14-15, NKJV)
7. You shall not leave the house of the Lord in disarray (Hag. 1:3-11, NKJV)
8. You shall not neglect your mission to bear fruit (Jn. 15:2 & 6, NKJV)
9. You shall not be remiss in the performance of your duties (Jer. 48:10, NAB)
10. You shall not rebel against the Church Administration (II Time 3:8, NIV)
Growing up in the Church of Christ, these commandments were engrained for as long as I can remember. I was taught that acting against any of these commandments was enough for me to not be 'saved.' For most of my youth, I would hyperanalyze every decision I made. I would question 'what would happen if someone in the Church saw me doing this?' It scares me because I see the repurcussions of those who are witnessed commiting sin. When I moved out, I decided to stop obeying a piece of paper and to start living my truth.
